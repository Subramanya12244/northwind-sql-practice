# Q17. Employees Above Average Revenue (Correlated Subquery)

**Category:** Correlated Subqueries
**Difficulty:** Medium

---

## Problem Statement

Same business need as Q7 — flag above-average-revenue employees — solved via `HAVING` and a nested subquery rather than a CTE.

## Objective

Return employees whose total revenue exceeds the average revenue across all employees, using a `HAVING` clause referencing a subquery.

## Tables Used

- `employees`
- `orders`
- `order_details`

## Expected Output

| Column Name | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| employee_id | Unique identifier of the employee |
| first_name | Employee's first name |
| last_name | Employee's last name |
| total_revenue | Sum of discounted revenue generated by this employee |

**Sample output:**

| employee_id | first_name | last_name | total_revenue |
|-------------|------------|-----------|---------------|
| 4 | Margaret | Peacock | 232890.85 |
| 3 | Janet | Leverling | 202812.84 |
| 1 | Nancy | Davolio | 192107.60 |

*(Sample values are illustrative, based on the standard Northwind dataset, and intended to show shape/format — not guaranteed to match your exact data instance.)*

## Concepts Used

- INNER JOIN (multi-table)
- GROUP BY
- HAVING
- Subquery (non-correlated)
- Aggregate Functions (SUM, AVG)

## Why This Approach

**Structurally identical to Q16, applied to employees instead of customers:** the same duplication trade-off applies — the revenue formula appears twice, once for the outer aggregate, once inside the inner subquery that computes the global average across all employees.

## Common Mistakes

- Same risk as Q16: letting the revenue logic drift between the two copies if the formula needs to change.
- Grouping by name instead of `employee_id`.

## Difficulty

**Medium**

## Interview Follow-up Questions

1. How would you refactor this and Q16 to avoid duplicating the revenue formula, while still avoiding a full CTE?
2. What does this query's execution plan likely look like compared to the CTE-based Q7?

## Learning Outcomes

- Reinforce the subquery-vs-CTE comparison pattern across a second entity, solidifying the conceptual transfer.
